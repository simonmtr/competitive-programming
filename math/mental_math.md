
# Mental Math



## Percentage Tricks

### Swap the numbers in a percentage calculation

x% of y = y% of x

Example: 20% of 50 = 50% of 20
= 10

Example: 15% of 80 = 80% of 15
= 12

### Find percentage of an easy divisor of 100
1. See that multiplying one number can easily give you 100
2. Find the percentage of that number
3. Multiply the percentage by the other number

Example: 25% of 80
25 x 4 = 100
25% of 4 = 1
1 x 80 = 80
Answer: 20

Example: 12.5% of 64
12.5 x 8 = 100
12.5% of 8 = 1
1 x 64 = 64
Answer: 8

### Move the decimal Rule

1. Move the decimal point in the percentage to the left to convert it to a decimal
2. Multiply the decimal by the number

Example: 6% of 90
Move the decimal point: 0.06
0.06 x 90 = 5.4
Answer: 5.4

Example: 0.5% of 200
Move the decimal point: 0.005
0.005 x 200 = 1
Answer: 1

### Calculate it out

1. Convert the percentage to a fraction
2. Multiply the fraction by the number

Example: 13% of 51
10 % of 51 = 5.1
1 % of 51 = 0.51 -> 3 % of 51 = 1.53
5.1 + 1.53 = 6.63
Answer: 6.63

## Multiplication Tricks

### Multiply any number by 11
1. Add the digits together
2. Place the sum between the digits
3. if the sum is greater than 9, carry the 1 to the next digit from left to right

Example: 23 x 11
2 + 3 = 5
Answer: 253

Example: 273 x 11
2 + 7 = 9
7 + 3 = 10 (carry the 1)
Answer: 3003

### Multiply two 2-digit numbers where the first digits are the same and the second digits ending with 5
1. Multiply the first digit by itself plus one
2. Add 25 to the end of the answer

Example: 75 x 75
7 x (7 + 1) = 56
Answer: 5625

Example: 1225 x 1225
12 x (12 + 1) = 156
Answer: 156225


### Multiply two 2-digit numbers where the first digits are the same and the second digits add up to 10
1. Multiply the first digit by itself plus one
2. Multiply the second digits together

Example: 44 x 46
4 x (4 + 1) = 20
4 x 6 = 24
Answer: 2024

Example: 123 x 127
12 x (12 + 1) = 156
3 x 7 = 21
Answer: 15621


### Multiply any 2 digit numbers up to 100
1. Multiply the first digits together
2. Multiply the second digits together
3. Add the products of the first and second digits together

Example: 50 x 49
5 x 4 = 20
0 x 9 = 0
-> 20_0
(5 x 9) + (0 x 4) = 45
Answer: 2450

Example: 32 x 31
3 x 3 = 9
2 x 1 = 2
-> 9_2
(3 x 1) + (2 x 3) = 9
Answer: 992

Example: 39 x 28
3 x 2 = 6
9 x 8 = 72
-> 6_72
(3 x 8) + (9 x 2) = 42
Answer: 1092

Example: 73 x 64
7 x 6 = 42
3 x 4 = 12
-> 42_12
(7 x 4) + (3 x 6) = 46
Answer: 4672

## Division Tricks

### Divide by 5

1. Multiply the number by 2
2. Move the decimal point one place to the left

Example: 125 ÷ 5
125 x 2 = 250
Move the decimal point: 25.0
Answer: 25

Example: 12304 ÷ 5
12304 x 2 = 24608
Move the decimal point: 2460.8
Answer: 2460.8

### Long Division
1. Find the largest multiple of the divisor that is less than or equal to the first digit(s) of the dividend
2. Remove the multiple from the dividend and bring down the next digit
3. Repeat the process until all digits have been brought down

Example: 208 ÷ 4
20 ÷ 4 = 5
8 ÷ 4 = 2
Answer: 52

Example: 1234 ÷ 5
12 ÷ 5 = 2 (remainder 2)
Bring down the next digit: 23
23 ÷ 5 = 4 (remainder 3)
Bring down the next digit: 34
34 ÷ 5 = 6 (remainder 4)
Answer: 246.8


## Addition Tricks

### Add large numbers using rounding
1. Round each number to the nearest ten, hundred, etc.
2. Add the rounded numbers together
3. Adjust the sum by adding or subtracting the difference between the original numbers and the rounded

Example: 497 + 368
Round 497 to 500 and 368 to 370
500 + 370 = 870
Adjust the sum: 870 - (3 + 2) = 865
Answer: 865

Example: 1234 + 5678
Round 1234 to 1200 and 5678 to 5700
1200 + 5700 = 6900
Adjust the sum: 6900 + (34 + 22) = 6956
Answer: 6956
